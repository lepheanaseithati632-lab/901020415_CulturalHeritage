Basotho Music & Dance

Liphotha

Youth dance at celebrations

Mohobelo

A powerful male dance symbolizing bravery and unity.

Mokhibo

Women's kneeling dance performed with graceful movements.

Ndlamo

A celebratory dance with stamping movements.

Tsikitlana

Performed by children, playful and rhythmic.

Mokorotlo

Performed with uluvations,usually in groups.

Phathisi

Dance involving ankle rattles for rhythm.

Maqekha

A special dance used during girls'initiation ceremonies.

Litolobonya

A dance and musical form primarily for women and girls.

Mokoropo

social dance at weddings.

Lesiba

Wind-powered musical blow(national insrunent)

Moropa

used in ceremonies and dances.

Thomo

Plucked mouth-resonated steing

Sekebeku

Percussion made from wood or metal.

Mamokhorong

String instrument like a violin

Setolo-tolo

Small mouth instrument

Lekolulo

Flute played by herdboys

Nts'iloana

Seed-filled rattle for rhythm

Phalaphala

Horn trumpet used for communication

Khomo ea metsi

Calabash drum played in rivers

Lipheku

Wooden clappers used during dancing

Choral accompaniment

Choir behind dancers singing and clapping, suppoorting the main dancers

Lithoko

Basotho praise poems

Seleteng
